## Overview & Objective

Port Royal is a push-your-luck card game for 2–5 players set in the bustling Caribbean harbor of Port Royal. Players act as merchants and pirates, drawing cards to populate the harbor display and then trading with ships or hiring crew to build their personal empires.

**Goal:** Accumulate the most **Influence points** by hiring Profession cards and completing Expeditions. The game ends as soon as one player reaches **12 Influence points**, and the player with the most Influence points at the end of the final round wins.

Coins are the currency of the game. Each Coin is represented by a card drawn face-down from the Play deck (the coin image is on the card back). Coins are spent to hire Professions.

---

## Components

- **120 Play Cards**, consisting of:
  - **50 Ships** (10 of each of 5 colors): Yellow Pinnace, Blue Flute, Green Barque, Red Frigate, Black Galleon
  - **60 Profession Cards**: 10 Traders (2 per color), 5 Settlers, 5 Captains, 5 Priests, 3 Jacks of all Trades, 10 Sailors, 3 Pirates, 4 Señoritas, 5 Jesters, 6 Admirals, 4 Governors
  - **6 Expeditions** (1 is used only in 5-player games)
  - **4 Tax Increase Cards**
- **4 Gambler Promo Cards** (marked with P)

## Setup

1. **Find the special Expedition card** (the only Play card without a Coin on its back).
   - With fewer than 5 players: return it to the box.
   - With exactly 5 players: place it face-up in the middle of the table now.
2. **Shuffle all remaining Play cards** face-down to form the **Play deck**. Leave space beside it for a face-up **discard pile**.
3. **Deal each player 3 cards** face-down into their personal display, coin-side up. These represent their starting 3 Coins.
4. Determine the **start player**: the last person who visited a body of water.

## Turn Structure

Each player in turn order becomes the **active player** and performs the following 2 phases:

1. **Phase 1: Discover** (mandatory)
2. **Phase 2: Trade & Hire** (may be forfeited)

After the active player completes Phase 2, all **other players (in clockwise order)** also get to take 1 card from the harbor display, paying the active player 1 Coin as compensation.

---

### Phase 1: Discover

The active player draws cards one at a time from the Play deck and places them face-up in the center of the table (the **harbor display**). After each card, they may choose to draw another or voluntarily stop and move to Phase 2.

**Forced stop (busting):** If the active player draws a Ship whose color **matches** a Ship already in the harbor display, and cannot or does not repel it, they must discard all cards currently in the harbor display (Expeditions are excepted — they remain on the table). Phase 2 is forfeited. Instead, **all players gain 1 Coin for each Jester** in their personal display. The active player may still fulfill Expeditions before ending their turn.

**Card types drawn during Discover:**

| Card Type | What Happens |
|---|---|
| Profession | Placed in the harbor display; available for hiring. |
| Ship | Placed in the harbor display OR repelled (discarded). |
| Expedition | Placed separately (not in the harbor display) until claimed. |
| Tax Increase | Resolved immediately, then discarded. |

**Repelling a Ship:** The active player may discard a newly drawn Ship instead of adding it to the harbor display, provided they have Sailors and/or Pirates in their display whose combined **Cutlasses** equal or exceed the Cutlass value printed on the Ship. Cutlasses are never spent — they can be used multiple times in a turn. You can never repel a Ship that has a special "cannot be repelled" symbol on it.

**Reshuffling:** Whenever the Play deck runs out, shuffle the discard pile to create a new face-down Play deck.

---

### Phase 2: Trade & Hire

The number of cards the active player may take is determined by the number of **differently colored Ships** in the harbor display:

| Ships of Different Colors | Cards Active Player May Take |
|---|---|
| 0–3 | 1 card |
| 4 | 2 cards |
| 5 | 3 cards |

Cards are taken **one at a time**, so the ability of a newly hired Profession becomes available immediately for the next card taken.

**Trade:** Take 1 Ship from the harbor display, place it in the discard pile, and gain Coins equal to the number printed on the Ship's banner.

**Hire:** Pay Coins equal to the Profession's cost (place those Coin cards face-up in the discard pile) and place the Profession in your personal display. Its ability is now permanently available.

**Non-active players:** After the active player takes their cards, each other player (clockwise) may take 1 card. Each must pay the **active player 1 Coin** as compensation. (When trading a Ship, this payment can come from the Ship's income.) Leftover cards are discarded after everyone has had their turn.

## Card Types

### Ships

There are 5 ship types (colors), each with varying Cutlass values and Coin payouts:

| Ship | Color | Cutlass Range | Coin Range |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pinnace | Yellow | 1–4 | 1–4 |
| Flute | Blue | 1–5 | 1–5 |
| Barque | Green | 1–5 | 1–5 |
| Frigate | Red | 1–6 | 1–6 |
| Galleon | Black | 2–7 | 2–7 |

Each Ship card shows the number of Coins gained when traded and the Cutlass strength required to repel it.

### Profession Cards

Professions have two parts:
- **Banner (top):** The permanent ability granted to the player.
- **Shield (right side):** The number of Influence points the card is worth.

Professions are placed in the player's personal display after being hired and their abilities stack (e.g., 2 Señoritas = 2 Coin discount on hiring).

### Expedition Cards

Expedition cards are placed separately from the harbor display when drawn. They show requirements (symbols on the bottom of the card) that must be met to claim them. Only the **active player** may claim Expeditions, and they can do so at any time during their turn.

### Tax Increase Cards

Resolved immediately when drawn. Two variants exist:
- **Max:** Players with 12+ Coins lose half (rounded down). Then the player with the **most Cutlasses** gains 1 Coin.
- **Min:** Players with 12+ Coins lose half (rounded down). Then the player with the **fewest Influence points** gains 1 Coin.

In both cases, if there is a tie, all tied players gain 1 Coin. After resolution, Tax Increase cards are discarded.

## Ships – How They Work

Ships in the harbor display are the primary source of income. When a player **trades** a Ship:

1. Take the Ship from the harbor display.
2. Place it in the discard pile.
3. Gain Coins equal to the number on the Ship's banner (draw that many cards face-down from the Play deck and add them to your personal display, coin-side up).

**Traders bonus:** If you own a Trader Profession matching the Ship's color, you gain 1 additional Coin per matching Trader when you trade with that Ship.

**Repelling Ships:** During Phase 1, the active player can repel a Ship (send it directly to the discard pile before it enters the harbor) by matching or exceeding its Cutlass value with Sailors and/or Pirates. This prevents it from potentially causing a bust later.

---

## Hiring – How It Works

To hire a Profession from the harbor display:

1. Pay the Coin cost shown on the bottom of the Profession card (place those Coin cards face-up in the discard pile).
2. Place the Profession in your personal display.
3. The ability is immediately active for subsequent actions that turn.

**Señorita discount:** Each Señorita in your display reduces the hire cost of any Profession by 1 Coin (minimum cost: 0 Coins).

Non-active players who hire from the harbor must also pay the **active player 1 Coin** as compensation.

## Profession Abilities Reference

### Expedition-Use Professions

| Profession | Count | Ability |
|---|---|---|
| Priest | 5 | Must be discarded to fulfill Expeditions requiring a Priest symbol. |
| Settler | 5 | Must be discarded to fulfill Expeditions requiring a Settler symbol. |
| Captain | 5 | Must be discarded to fulfill Expeditions requiring a Captain symbol. |
| Jack of all Trades | 3 | Wild card: can substitute for any Priest, Settler, or Captain when completing Expeditions. |

### Income & Bonus Professions

| Profession | Count | Ability |
|---|---|---|
| Trader (per color) | 2 each | Gain 1 extra Coin when trading a Ship of the matching color. |
| Admiral | 6 | At the start of your Phase 2 turn, if there are 5+ cards in the harbor display, immediately gain 2 Coins per Admiral. (Newly hired Admirals do not trigger immediately.) |
| Jester | 5 | If the harbor display is empty at the start of Phase 2, gain 1 Coin per Jester. If Phase 2 is skipped (bust), all players gain 1 Coin per Jester. |
| Governor | 4 | During Phase 2, may take 1 extra card per Governor (non-active players still pay 1 Coin per extra card). |
| Señorita | 4 | Reduces hire cost by 1 Coin per Señorita (minimum 0). |

### Combat Professions

| Profession | Count | Ability |
|---|---|---|
| Sailor | 10 | Provides 1 Cutlass for repelling Ships. |
| Pirate | 3 | Provides 2 Cutlasses for repelling Ships. |

### Promo Card

| Profession | Count | Ability |
|---|---|---|
| Gambler | 4 (P) | Once per Discover phase, draw 4 cards simultaneously (you cannot repel ships but do resolve Tax Increases). If 2+ ships share a color, you bust. If all ships are different colors, end Phase 1 and gain 1 additional card during Phase 2. Multiple Gamblers can be chained. |

## Expeditions – How They Work

- When an Expedition card is drawn during Phase 1, it is placed **apart** from the harbor display on the table. It stays there until claimed.
- **Only the active player** may claim Expeditions, at any time during their turn (even after busting).
- To claim an Expedition, the active player discards Profession cards from their display that match the symbols required on the Expedition (e.g., 1 Priest + 1 Captain). The **Jack of all Trades** can substitute for any single Expedition requirement.
- Upon claiming the Expedition: take the Expedition card and add it to your personal display (it is worth its printed Influence points). Draw Coins from the Play deck equal to the number shown on the Expedition.
- A player may claim as many Expeditions as they can fulfill in a single turn.
- Expeditions are **not** discarded if the active player busts during Phase 1; they remain on the table.

---

## Scoring & Influence Points

Influence points are tracked by the cards in your personal display:
- Each hired **Profession card** shows a shield with its Influence point value.
- Each **Expedition card** claimed is worth its printed Influence points.

---

## End Game Conditions

The game end is triggered as soon as **any player reaches 12 Influence points** in their personal display. Play continues through the current round until the player to the **right of the start player** has had a turn as the active player.

**Winner:** The player with the most Influence points. Tiebreaker: the tied player with the most Coins. If still tied, those players share the victory.

---

## End of Game Variant

An optional variant changes the end condition: the game ends when a player has **12 Influence points AND at least 1 Expedition** in their personal display. The winner must also have at least 1 Expedition; ties are resolved identically to the standard game.
